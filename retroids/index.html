Description

Retroids is a classic arcade space shooter built entirely in HTML5 and JavaScript. It is a faithful homage to the 1979 classic, Asteroids, but with new, modern challenges. The player controls a spaceship in a dangerous asteroid field and must destroy asteroids, UFOs, and mines while avoiding collisions. The game features realistic physics, a progressive level system, and easily customizable sound effects.

This webpage and its content, along with the game itself, was created with Gemini AI as a test of the capabilities of 2.5 Pro

How to Play

Rotate Left:

Use the `Left Arrow` Key to turn your ship counter-clockwise.

Rotate Right:

Use the `Right Arrow` Key to turn your ship clockwise.

Thrust Forward:

Use the `Up Arrow` Key to propel your ship in the direction it's pointing.

Shoot:

Press the `Spacebar` to fire your laser cannon.

Hyperspace:

Press the `Down Arrow` Key for an emergency jump to a random location. Use with caution!

Core Features

UFO Enemies

Starting from level 2, hostile UFOs will appear, firing projectiles at the player.

Dynamic Minefields

Higher levels have a chance to become dangerous minefields with proximity-activated mines.

Advanced Physics

Asteroids realistically bounce off each other, and can be destroyed by colliding with mines.

Inertia-Based Movement

The ship has momentum and will drift through space. Master your thrusters to survive.

Level Progression

Clearing all threats advances you to the next, more difficult level.

Custom Audio

The game uses `.wav` files for sound, which can be easily replaced by the user.

Setup & Customization

To hear the sound effects, you must place your audio files in the same folder as the game's HTML file. The game looks for the following specific filenames:

  • shoot.wav
  • explosion.wav
  • player_death.wav
  • thrust.wav
  • hyperspace.wav
  • ufo_shoot.wav
  • ufo_destroyed.wav
  • mine_explosion.wav

Developer Notes

The game's logic is contained within the `<script>` tag in the game's HTML file. Key game parameters can be easily tweaked by changing the constant variables at the top of the script, such as `FRICTION`, `SHIP_THRUST`, and `PROJECTILE_SPEED`.
